Which courses encouraged you to identify theoretical, conceptual, or methodological connections between your chosen concentration disciplines? How did they encourage this identification ( through subject matter, readings, lectures, course work, project work, mentorship, etc.)?

Interactive Art and Computational Design, Experimental Capture and Physical Computing helped me make connections between art and computer science. Although these courses didn’t involve any of the theoretical aspects of computer science, they nevertheless gave me the chance to apply programming skills toward the creation of artworks. All the assignments in these courses required programming.

Which courses helped you to apply these connections and integrate or combine your concentrations (through experiments, exercises, research projects, creative projects, etc.)? What assignments allowed you to produce this kind of interdisciplinary work?

Since I often work with code as a material, I was able to integrate my knowledge of programming with artistic projects that I pursued in Contextual Practice, Urban Intervention, Sound Art and Digitally Mediated Performance. In this sense, most of the assignments I did for these courses were interdisciplinary.

In which courses do you feel you improved your communication skills, either in written, visual, or oral expression? Which assignments show most clearly your skills in this area?

Through regular written assignments, I was able to practice my writing skills in the Philosophy of Social Science, Modern Visual Culture, and Aesthetics of Dysfunction. I was able to practice my oral skills in preparation for my sophomore review in Contextual Practice. The essays that I wrote in Aesthetics of Dysfunction and Modern Visual Culture most clearly show my development as a writer.

Which courses allowed you to explore local and global perspectives on culture, society, and history? Which assignments pushed you to investigate and expand these perspectives?

Contextual Practice encouraged me to consider the contextual aspects of artmaking: how an artwork relates to its site and audience or participants. The assignments for this course required that we make work in response to specific contexts, which inevitably raises questions of society, culture and history. Contextual practice

In which courses were you able to engage in both theoretical and practical work to produce real-world knowledge? How did the assignments for these courses encourage the application of theory to practical knowledge?

The interdisciplinary courses I’ve taken have all been project based, that is, they required me to produce artworks rather than any kind of applicable, real-world knowledge.

Which courses encouraged you to think more about how decision-making on both the individual and social levels is informed by cognitive, behavioral, societal, and ethical issues? Which assignments allowed you to more closely examine how these issues affect decisions?

Philosophy of Social Science explored how social scientists model human decision- making. The assignments focused on the philosophical implications on various approaches to understanding decision-making in social science.

In which courses did you encounter hardship, difficulty, or struggle with course material or assignments? Which assignments challenged you (intellectually, personally) the most?

Courses in computer science which involved proof-based mathematics challenged me the most. Assignments in which I had to carefully reason about the correctness of particular proposition proved to be challenging for me.

What areas do you feel are undeveloped or have been untouched by your previous, to- date course experiences? If so, why do you think that is? (Lack of opportunity/time constraints, lack of options, etc.) Are there courses you still wish to take that will allow you to achieve the kinds of opportunities for integration and application of your concentrations as outlined above? If you believe there is no such class at CMU, what kind of courses do you think would have fostered these experiences?

I wish to take courses in the humanities that involve research and writing. Most of my courses have involved art-making or analytical reasoning, but very few have focused on theoretical reasoning, research and argumentation. I feel that my education could be greatly enriched by humanities courses.
